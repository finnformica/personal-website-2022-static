Welcome!

I am a Mechanical and Electrical engineer recently graduated from the University of Bristol in July 2022. I have a love for programming - especially within the fields of Data Science, Artificial Intelligence, and Web Development. The fields of Economics, Finance, and Cryptocurrency also interest me greatly and have pushed me to pursue the Financial Technology with Data Science MSc at the University of Bristol. Most recently, I have been researching the implementation of a Fuzzy Logic Control system within an 800V electric vehicle utilising simulation-based models.

At this early stage in my life I am looking to expand my knowledge and get involved with anything at all, I always love an interesting challenge. I am currently interested in getting involved with Blockchain Technology and developing decentralised apps (dApps) using Solidity.

I am also...

  • an avid boulderer and have been looking to progress to outdoor lead and bouldering.
  • one of those folks attempting to trade the Stock market and Crypto market; however, with a recession looming the conditions are less than favourable.
  • learning how to create "art" pieces using JavaScript.

Higher Education

Financial Technology with Data Science MSc (2022 - now)

After my engineering degree I decided that the Mechanical and Electrical engineering fields were not ones that I wished to pursure. I had a growing interest in software that stemmed from my love of robotics as a kid, and I realised that this MSc combined several interests of mine into a single qualification; particularly Finance and Cryptocurrency. Off the back of this MSc I hope to build my knowledge within the field of Data Science and gather the skills required to become a Blockchain Developer.

Mechanical and Electrical Engeering BEng (2019 - 2022)

I chose this degree to pursue a career in robotics, deeply inspired by the works of Boston Dynamics and such. However, it did not meet my expectations, and the focus was more on sustainability and the electrification of the grid. The highlight of my degree was my independent research project. With the recent introduction of 800V electric vehicle powertrains on the market my research looked into some viable control schemes for these EV powertrain architectures. I developed a Fuzzy Logic Control scheme to effectively reduce the torque and current ripple within the electric drive.
